It's not just the airline fares you can check, but also flight booking information. Suppose you plan to arrive at your destination at 2 p.m. and American Airlines shows all flights arriving at that time for $300, wouldn't you rather quickly check another airline that also arrives at 2 p.m. but for $150?

It's probably much cheaper if you fly Airline A, then connect to Airline B, and finally to Airline C. OTAs (and traditional travel agents) will figure out the lowest price for such an itinerary, while the airline's website will let you stay in their own system. Even metasearch sites like Tripadvisor.com, Flights and Kayak will only recommend booking with Expedia.com or Orbitz if the best option is to fly Canadian Top Air on the way there and US Airways on the way back.
